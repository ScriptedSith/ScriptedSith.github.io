Sith Imperial Policy

Violation of TOS

Cybercrimes
Any and all cybercriminal activity, including IP logging, doxxing, DDOSing, Discord raiding, illicit or predatory behavior, or any and all other cyber crimes will result in a permanent blacklist from any and all TSB-affiliated games.

Exploiting
Any and all exploits or any nature of hacking are not permitted within any TSB-affiliated games and will result in a permanent blacklist.

Filters
Bypassing filters by using another word to substitute for an otherwise filter word.

High Treason

High Treason is defined as the action of betraying someone or something.

Attempting to spy for another group, circumvent authority, usurp power, using position to push another's agenda, or the unlawful leakage of non-public information will result in a permanent blacklist.

Destroying any property of The Sith Brotherhood.

Administrative Regulations

Divisional High Ranks, Sith Overseer+, and DC+ are the only ranks that may have the use of Admin.

If anyone else is found to have admin, the person who gave the admin and the person who is unauthorized to have it will be permanently barred from admin commands and will be demoted to Acolyte.

Admins are to ask permission before using admin commands on those higher ranked than them.

Any and all admin abuse is to be reported to any High Command member.

Conflict against Sith/Visitors

No person is allowed to attack Sith/Visitors unless they are hostile.

A Sith member may attack another Sith if:

They are defending themselves from being attacked.

Have initiated a duel with another person.

If they are in a division and are given a command to execute said person.

Mandate of Respect

All members of the Brotherhood are to respect one another, regardless of rank.

No person is allowed to disrespect, make fun of, or attempt to defame any division, divisional member, or divisional overseers.

All Sith must use respectful titles to refer to those considered as High Ranks.

High Ranks constitute as anyone above the rank of Sith Overseer+

These high ranks that were defined are to be titled as: "[RANK] [NAME]"

All Sith must use respectful titles to refer to those considered high-rank.

All Sith must use the proper etiquette in greeting their masters and DC+

It is presumed that the Master informs the Apprentice to kneel upon his arrival, or whatever the Master presumes worthy of a greeting.

All Sith must Bow to DC+ and Kneel to PB+

Only Divisional Members are to Bow to only their divisional DC and no other DC member.

Visitors, Architects, The Force, and The Emperor are not required to bow or kneel for anyone

If the Master or DC does not say rise or acknowledge you within 15 seconds, Sith are allowed to rise on their own accord.

Insubordination

All Brotherhood members are to listen and follow any and all commands given to them by a High Rank.

A Brotherhood member may disregard the command if/when

It breaks any of the laws within the IP.

Is harmful to others/yourself.

Breaks a set rule within your division.

A High Rank's command may be overruled if someone Higher than them, or your divisional overseer, gives another command.

Force Laws

Force is not to be used while dueling. This includes fending off hostiles.

Hostiles who use the Force on visitors or disrupt training count as force abusers.

You may use the Force on hostiles who use it on you first. You may only use the force on them until they die.

Force may be used to kill anyone who enters a training without permission from the host.

DC+ may use the Force on people who disrespect them. This only includes ranks under them.

Powerbase+ may use the Force on non-hostiles that attack them. This only includes ranks under them.

Breaking the following rules will result in punishment. Any Force Abuser is to be reported to Powerbase+.

Sith Regulations

Grammar and Maturity are required from all the ranks above Sith Tyro.

All members of the Brotherhood are to wear their correct uniforms.

Divisional members that go off duty must wear the designated Sith uniform or anything that is Sith-like in attire.

Only Sith Master+, Divisional HRs, and DC+ may be at ease.

You may not be at ease if someone of a higher rank is nearby.

All members are to not have misshapen heads or any t-shirts that obstruct the uniform.

In order to spectate a training, you must get permission from the host to spectate.

Dark Council+ are always allowed to spectate.

Sith are not allowed on the mats unless they are dueling somebody.

Divisional Regulations

In order to join any division you must be the rank of Acolyte

Divisional members are to follow the command of their Overseers and HRs.

No other Sith member/Divisional Overseer/Dark Councilor besides Powerbase+ may command another division.

Sith are only allowed to be in one division.

Divisional Discharge:

Honorable Discharge:

Served in the division for more than two weeks

Demonstrated meritorious service in the division

Sith that are Honorably Discharged are free to join another division whenever they wish no wait time.

General Discharge:

Served in the division for two weeks

Sith that are given a General Discharge are free to join another division after 2 days have passed.

Dishonorable Discharge:

Left before the two-week commitment/purged from the division

Sith that are Dishonorably Discharged will be barred from joining any divisions for three weeks and blacklisted from the division permanently.

Inquisitors, Dark Honor Guards, and Imperial Intelligence are exempt from the Conflict against Sith/Visitors law.

No one will interfere in the duties of Imperial Intelligence, Dark Honor Guards, or Inquisitors.

High-Rank Regulations

All High Ranks must show maturity, competence, and diligence within their rank and duty.

No High Ranks are allowed to malinger in their duties without facing the consequences.

Malinger is defined as feigning ill to escape any and all duties.

Inactivity notices posted back to back will result in a form of investigation.

If the person in question is found to be faking their inactivity notices, they will be permanently barred from any High Ranks within the Brotherhood until they are deemed ready enough to accept such title again.

No High Ranks should promote bias or reward any form of bribery or personal gain.

Bribery is defined as the acceptance or solicitation of any value (money, in-game currency, ranks) in exchange for something else.

Any High Rank found to commit bribery will be permanently barred from any High Ranks within the Brotherhood until they are deemed ready enough to accept such title again.

Bias is defined as inclination or prejudice for or against one person or a group.

No High Ranks should use any abusive commands in any game affiliated with The Sith Brotherhood.

Only Powerbase+ may permit the usage of certain commands.

No High Ranks, divisional or main, should leak any information, intentional or unintentional, that is regarded within the HR Communications in the discord.

Kaggaths

A Kaggath is an ancient rite of the Sith in which those can elevate their status if they believe another Sith is incompetent or not fit for their position.
The minimum rank to Kaggath, a Sith member, is Acolyte+.

Sith may Kaggath up to those three ranks above them to swap ranks.

This rule does not apply to divisional kaggaths.

In the case of someone challenging an HR, the person doing the Kaggath must show:

Legitimate Reason for the Kaggath

Legitimate Reasons include:

Inactivity without notice

Constant breakage of the Sith Imperial Policy

Failure to uphold their duties, etc.

A relatively clean track record

This applies to both Discord and affiliated games.

If the record shows that a punishment was inflicted a minimum of 3 months ago, they will not count towards the track record.

Competence and ability to uphold duty as an HR

Only the Dark Council may Kaggath any Powerbase Positions

All parties of a Kaggath must agree to the terms of the others.

If the other party does not uphold the agreement, they will forfeit their position automatically and will be given to the other party.

The terms of the Kaggaths must be reasonable.
